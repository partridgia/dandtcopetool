HOW IT WORKS
Phase 1
Citizens choose to enter one of the C.O.P.E. contracts which have a minimum consensus level of 69 combined (or current population minus 39). Contract A (C.O.P.E. Citizens) is for those who wish to retain one of the final 69 NFTs but take a smaller treasury share. Contract B (C.O.P.E. Evaders) is for those willing to take a larger treasury share but let their Citizen die and have an Evader returned. For the purpose of C.O.P.E. NFTs are assumed to have a value of 3 ETH (TBC) so Contract B participants would receive 3 ETH (TBC) more than Contract A participants. The IRS runs an automatic bailout schedule for all citizens in either contract from the moment they join — this encourages participation and minimises contract complexity.
Phase 2
After consensus is reached, Contract A remains open for an undisclosed length of time for additional joiners to the collective, increasing the treasury share for everyone in the collective. Remaining citizens outside the C.O.P.E. contracts would be paying daily taxes at a loss and therefore nudged to join. Contract B is closed to new entrants.
Phase 3
Once C.O.P.E. the contract is closed (on an Epoch change), the Contract B Citizens are delinquent and killed by the IRS. Contract A claims proportions of the game treasury for all citizens within it, this is then split in accordance with the agreed structure with those participants in Contract B.
Phase 4
With all treasury claimed and allocated, NFTs and treasury shares are redeemed by or returned to their owners. On exit there will be an option to collect an exclusive participants edition by m0dest for 0.969 via an allowlisted mint page (allocation of 1 per NFT entered).
THE C.O.P.E. GAME
What is this?
Death and Taxes is a game of survival. Citizens die. The last 69 standing share the treasury. But what if you could negotiate your fate instead of waiting for the taxman?
The offer
The C.O.P.E. settlement contract lets a group of citizens agree to resolve the game together — before the natural end. You deposit your citizen NFT and collectively decide who lives and who is left to die. In return, everyone in the settlement gets a guaranteed share of the treasury.
JOINING THE C.O.P.E. GAME
How to join
Each C.O.P.E. game opens at 00:00 UTC and runs for 42 hours based on a defined minimum participant number. If you would be happy to exit the Death and Taxes game based on the minimum treasury split at resolution, transfer your citizen NFT into the contract during this window. If citizens die in the main game during the period, NFT slots increase accordingly.
Holding multiple NFTs?
If you hold more than one citizen NFT, consider entering a proportion of them into the C.O.P.E. game. Any citizens you keep outside the game remain yours — if the game resolves, those citizens will be among the final 69 survivors and you will receive a full treasury share and retain the NFT for each one. Entering some and keeping others lets you benefit from both the guaranteed settlement payout and the natural game resolution.
Tax status on entry
Your tax status is checked at the point of deposit. For the first 24 hours of a game, your taxes must be fully current. In the final 18 hours, participants with outstanding taxes due that same day are also permitted entry — you will be returned before midnight UTC and will have time to pay before becoming delinquent. If you are unable to pay taxes before being audited after returning, we suggest contacting m0dest directly to request a bailout.

KEEPER SLOTS AND BIDDING
How keeper slots work
Keeper slots are calculated live throughout the game: 69 minus the number of citizens not in the C.O.P.E. game. As more citizens die in the main game, keeper slots increase — they never decrease. Slots are filled by earliest depositors first. If you are in a keeper slot and a higher bidder takes it, you move down to sacrificer status.
Bidding for a keeper slot
If you want to secure a keeper slot regardless of deposit order, you can place a bid — the amount of ETH you are willing to forgo from your treasury share in exchange for keeping your citizen NFT. Bids are irrevocable once placed. No ETH is sent upfront — your bid is an on-chain commitment deducted from your treasury share at resolution. Minimum bid is 1 ETH in increments of 0.25 ETH.
The clearing price
When the game locks, keeper slots are awarded to the highest bidders. All winning bidders pay the same price — the lowest winning bid. This is called the clearing price. If there are 13 keeper slots and 20 bidders, the 13 highest bids win and all 13 pay the 13th highest bid amount. The forfeited ETH from all winning bids at the clearing price is added to the sacrificer pool — increasing the payout for those who sacrifice.
HOW THE C.O.P.E. GAME RESOLVES
If the target is met
If enough participants deposit to meet the game target, the contract locks immediately at that block. No new deposits are accepted. Sacrificers remain exposed to the game's kill function and will be burned in due course. Keeper NFTs are protected during this waiting period. Once total citizen supply reaches 69, the C.O.P.E. game pulls each NFT's share of the main treasury. Participants then claim their own NFT and ETH share directly from the contract — keepers claim their citizen NFT and ETH, sacrificers claim their evader NFT and ETH.
The waiting period
Upon resolution, some NFTs within the C.O.P.E. contract may have up to 7 epochs of taxes pre-paid. The game cannot complete until those citizens become delinquent, are audited, and are killed by the main game. During this period, citizen NFTs outside the contract may trade at a premium reflecting the treasury that can be claimed — but it will not be possible to return any NFTs to participants until the treasury share has been claimed and distribution is complete. For those NFTs which would become part of the final 69, bailouts will be sought from m0dest or taxes will be paid on their behalf.
If the target is not met
If the game closes at 18:00 UTC on day 2 without reaching its target, all citizen NFTs are returned to their original wallets. There is no penalty. Each game attempt is fully independent.
Planned game schedule
C.O.P.E. games are launched with a target participant count and run for 42 hours each. If a game does not resolve, a new game can be launched with the same or a different target. The planned schedule will be published on this site ahead of each game. Targets may increase over time — later games with higher targets mean more sacrificers, a larger pool, and higher payouts for all participants.

CONTRACT FUNCTIONS
WRITE FUNCTIONS — OWNER ONLY
startGame(target, durationHours)
Launches a new C.O.P.E. game. Sets the participant target and duration. Pass 0 for durationHours to use the default 42 hours. Target must be greater than current citizen supply minus 68 to ensure at least one treasury claim is possible. Contract must be in IDLE state.
recordEvader(citizenTokenId, evaderTokenId)
Records the association between a burned citizen token ID and the evader token ID minted in the same transaction. Must be called after each sacrificer citizen is killed by the main game. Verifies the evader NFT is now held by this contract before recording.
withdrawTreasury(tokenIds[])
Calls the treasury claim function on behalf of each keeper token ID provided. Accumulates ETH into the contract balance. Can be called in batches to manage gas costs. Once complete, moves contract to RESOLVED state and participants can claim.
payTax(tokenId)
Pays outstanding taxes for a keeper token from the contract's ETH balance. Used during the waiting period to protect keeper NFTs from being killed if a tax bailout from m0dest has not been agreed. Owner should deposit a tax reserve into the contract before using this function.
abortAndReturn()
Aborts the current game and returns all escrowed citizen NFTs to their original depositor wallets. Any evader NFTs already received are also returned. Any ETH held in the contract is sent to the contract owner. Cannot be called once the contract is in RESOLVED state.
emergencyWithdrawETH(recipient)
Transfers all ETH held in the contract to a specified address. Safety function for recovering stuck funds. Owner only.
WRITE FUNCTIONS — PARTICIPANTS
depositNFT(tokenId)
Deposits a citizen NFT into the C.O.P.E. contract. You must own the token and your taxes must be current. The contract assigns your keeper or sacrificer status automatically based on deposit order. If your deposit causes the participant count to reach the game target, the contract locks immediately.
placeBid(tokenId, bidAmount)
Places an irrevocable bid for a keeper slot on a token you have deposited. Bid amount must be at least 1 ETH in increments of 0.25 ETH. No ETH is sent — the bid amount is deducted from your treasury share at resolution. Only one bid can be placed per token.
claim(tokenId, tipBps)
Claims your NFT and ETH share after resolution. Must be called by the original depositor wallet. Tip options are 0 (no tip), 500 (5%), 1000 (10%), or 2000 (20%) basis points. Keepers receive their citizen NFT and ETH. Sacrificers receive their evader NFT and ETH. Tip is sent to the contract creator in the same transaction.
checkExpiry()
Callable by anyone. Checks whether the 42 hour game window has elapsed without hitting the target. If so, moves the contract to expired state and returns all NFTs to original depositor wallets automatically.
READ FUNCTIONS
keeperSlots()
Returns the number of keeper slots currently available. Calculated live as 69 minus the number of citizens not in the C.O.P.E. game. Updates automatically as citizens die in the main game or new participants join.
currentKeepers()
Returns an array of token IDs currently allocated as keepers, ordered by priority — highest bidders first, then earliest depositors. Shows the live keeper queue at any point during the game.
isKeeper(tokenId)
Returns true or false for a given token ID. Tells you whether a specific citizen NFT is currently allocated a keeper slot.
previewShare(tokenId)
Returns the estimated ETH share for a given token ID based on current contract state. Keepers receive their treasury share minus the clearing price. Sacrificers receive their treasury share plus a portion of redistributed clearing price ETH.
getDepositedTokens()
Returns an array of all citizen token IDs currently deposited in the contract for the active game.
depositor(tokenId)
Returns the wallet address that originally deposited a given token ID. Used to verify ownership for claims and bid placement.
depositTime(tokenId)
Returns the timestamp of when a given token ID was deposited. Used to determine keeper slot allocation order for non-bidding participants.
bid(tokenId)
Returns the bid amount in ETH for a given token ID. Returns 0 if no bid has been placed.
clearingPrice()
Returns the current clearing price — the lowest winning bid given the current number of keeper slots and bids placed. Updates live as new bids are placed.
claimed(tokenId)
Returns true or false. Tells you whether a participant has already claimed their NFT and ETH share for a given token ID after resolution.
citizenToEvader(citizenTokenId)
Returns the evader token ID associated with a burned citizen token ID. Only populated after the contract owner has recorded the burn/mint association for a sacrificed citizen.
state()
Returns the current state of the contract. 0 = IDLE, 1 = OPEN, 2 = LOCKED, 3 = RESOLVED, 4 = EXPIRED.
gameId()
Returns the current game number. Increments by 1 each time a new game is started.
gameTarget()
Returns the participant target set for the current game.
gameStart()
Returns the Unix timestamp of when the current game started. Add 151200 (42 hours in seconds) to get the expiry time.
participantCount()
Returns the number of citizen NFTs currently deposited in the active game.
totalTreasuryETH()
Returns the total ETH pulled from the main treasury after resolution. Zero until withdrawTreasury() has been called.
treasuryWithdrawn()
Returns true once the treasury ETH has been pulled into the contract. Confirms that claims are ready to be made.
