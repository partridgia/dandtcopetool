THE C.O.P.E. GAME
What is this?
Death and Taxes is a game of survival. Citizens die. The last 69 standing share the treasury. But what if you could negotiate your fate instead of waiting for the taxman?
The offer
The C.O.P.E. settlement contract lets a group of citizens agree to resolve the game together — before the natural end. You deposit your citizen NFT and collectively decide who lives and who is left to die. In return, everyone in the settlement gets a guaranteed share of the treasury.
JOINING THE C.O.P.E. GAME
How to join
Each C.O.P.E. game opens at 00:00 UTC and runs for 42 hours based on a defined minimum participant number. If you would be happy to exit the Death and Taxes game based on the minimum treasury split at resolution, transfer your citizen NFT into the contract during this window. If citizens die in the main game during the period, NFT slots increase accordingly.
Holding multiple NFTs?
If you hold more than one citizen NFT, consider entering a proportion of them into the C.O.P.E. game. Any citizens you keep outside the game remain yours — if the game resolves, those citizens will be among the final 69 survivors and you will receive a full treasury share and retain the NFT for each one. Entering some and keeping others lets you benefit from both the guaranteed settlement payout and the natural game resolution.
Tax status on entry
Your tax status is checked at the point of deposit. For the first 24 hours of a game, your taxes must be fully current. In the final 18 hours, participants with outstanding taxes due that same day are also permitted entry — you will be returned before midnight UTC and will have time to pay before becoming delinquent. If you are unable to pay taxes before being audited after returning, we suggest contacting m0dest directly to request a bailout.

KEEPER SLOTS AND BIDDING
How keeper slots work
Keeper slots are calculated live throughout the game: 69 minus the number of citizens not in the C.O.P.E. game. As more citizens die in the main game, keeper slots increase — they never decrease. Slots are filled by earliest depositors first. If you are in a keeper slot and a higher bidder takes it, you move down to sacrificer status.
Bidding for a keeper slot
If you want to secure a keeper slot regardless of deposit order, you can place a bid — the amount of ETH you are willing to forgo from your treasury share in exchange for keeping your citizen NFT. Bids are irrevocable once placed. No ETH is sent upfront — your bid is an on-chain commitment deducted from your treasury share at resolution. Minimum bid is 1 ETH in increments of 0.25 ETH.
The clearing price
When the game locks, keeper slots are awarded to the highest bidders. All winning bidders pay the same price — the lowest winning bid. This is called the clearing price. If there are 13 keeper slots and 20 bidders, the 13 highest bids win and all 13 pay the 13th highest bid amount. The forfeited ETH from all winning bids at the clearing price is added to the sacrificer pool — increasing the payout for those who sacrifice.
HOW THE C.O.P.E. GAME RESOLVES
If the target is met
If enough participants deposit to meet the game target, the contract locks immediately at that block. No new deposits are accepted. Sacrificers remain exposed to the game's kill function and will be burned in due course. Keeper NFTs are protected during this waiting period. Once total citizen supply reaches 69, the C.O.P.E. game pulls each NFT's share of the main treasury. Participants then claim their own NFT and ETH share directly from the contract — keepers claim their citizen NFT and ETH, sacrificers claim their evader NFT and ETH.
The waiting period
Upon resolution, some NFTs within the C.O.P.E. contract may have up to 7 epochs of taxes pre-paid. The game cannot complete until those citizens become delinquent, are audited, and are killed by the main game. During this period, citizen NFTs outside the contract may trade at a premium reflecting the treasury that can be claimed — but it will not be possible to return any NFTs to participants until the treasury share has been claimed and distribution is complete. For those NFTs which would become part of the final 69, bailouts will be sought from m0dest or taxes will be paid on their behalf.
If the target is not met
If the game closes at 18:00 UTC on day 2 without reaching its target, all citizen NFTs are returned to their original wallets. There is no penalty. Each game attempt is fully independent.
Planned game schedule
C.O.P.E. games are launched with a target participant count and run for 42 hours each. If a game does not resolve, a new game can be launched with the same or a different target. The planned schedule will be published on this site ahead of each game. Targets may increase over time — later games with higher targets mean more sacrificers, a larger pool, and higher payouts for all participants.
